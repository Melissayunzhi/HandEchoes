In *Write a Word in the Air*, movement becomes mark.
Instead of ink, the gesture itself is captured — alive, shifting, and fleeting.

Inspired by Chinese calligraphy and José Abad Lorente’s *The Linking Line*,
the work explores how words dissolve into images, and meaning transforms in motion.

Here, writing is not a final act but an unfolding process.
With a wave of the hand, marks appear; with a press of ENTER, they are set into evolution —
drifting, multiplying, reshaping like memories carried on air.

*Write a Word in the Air* invites a dance between body and language,
offering new ways to feel, imagine, and embody text beyond the static page.
